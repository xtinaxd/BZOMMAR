The Palace, c. 1749 Plate 01

Perched above the Bay of Jounieh, Bzommar Palace was built in 1749 as a sanctuary of stone, light, and silence. Nearly three centuries later, it remains what it has always been — a place apart.

A Timeline

Moments that shaped us

1749
Foundation

Commissioned by the Boutros family, the palace is raised from local limestone — its archways hand-carved by master masons from Deir el Qamar.

The east wing is expanded to host visiting dignitaries. The cedar library — still intact — is added, housing over 4,000 volumes.

1881
The Eastern Wing
1923
The Gardens

French landscape architect Édouard Redont designs the formal terraces, olive groves, and the rose walk that still frames the afternoon light.

After decades as a private residence, the palace opens its doors to guests for the first time — with twelve rooms and a single restaurant.

1987
A House Becomes a Home
2024
The Renewal

A three-year restoration returns the palace to its original proportions — with every stone preserved, and every comfort quietly modernized.

Our Principles

What we hold
dear

Four quiet commitments that have guided Bzommar since its first guests crossed the threshold. They appear nowhere in a brochure, and yet shape every detail of your stay.

I.

Quiet Craft

Excellence that asks for no attention. The folded corner of a napkin. The second cup of coffee that arrives unbidden.

II.

Living Heritage

A palace is not a monument. We restore, we inhabit, we pass it on — lightly, so others may do the same.

III.

Of this Land

Stone from nearby quarries. Olive oil from our own groves. Every guest leaves with a taste of Mount Lebanon.

IV.

Unhurried Time

The most generous thing a hotel can offer is space in the hour. We do not crowd your day.

i.
Booking & Arrival

For weekends and the summer months (June–September), we recommend booking three to six weeks ahead. For private events and the festive season, twelve weeks is advisable.

Bookings made directly with the hotel are fully refundable up to 72 hours before arrival. Package stays and event reservations follow their own terms — please see our Policies.

Complimentary private transfers to and from Beirut-Rafic Hariri International are offered on bookings of two nights or more made directly through the hotel.

ii.
Amenities & Experience

Yes — Tilal Bzommar welcomes outside guests for dinner Wednesday through Sunday, and Sunday lunch. Reservations are required; please call ahead.

Well-behaved small pets are welcome in select rooms for a nominal fee, subject to availability. Please notify us at the time of booking.

iii.
Private Events

For weddings, family milestones, and private retreats, exclusive use of the palace is possible with a minimum three-night stay. Our Head of Heritage & Events will guide you through every detail.

The Grand Ballroom accommodates 300 guests seated for dinner and 450 for a cocktail reception. The adjoining terrace and gardens add space for up to 500 guests combined.

House Rules

Our policies, written plainly

A summary, not legal counsel. For full terms, please see our reservation agreement.

§ 01

Cancellation

Direct bookings may be cancelled without charge up to 72 hours before the arrival date. Within 72 hours, the first night's stay is payable. Package reservations, non-refundable rates, and group bookings follow individual terms disclosed at the time of booking.

In the event of unforeseen circumstances — illness, travel disruption — please write to us. We handle each case with discretion and good faith.

§ 02

Privacy

We collect only what is necessary to welcome you well: your name, contact details, and preferences you choose to share. We do not sell, rent, or trade your information. Our newsletter is opt-in and unsubscribing takes one click.

Guest records are retained for seven years, in compliance with Lebanese hospitality regulations, and then permanently deleted. Full details are available in our data-protection statement.

§ 03

Terms of Stay

  • i.Check-in is from 3:00 pm. Check-out is by 12:00 noon. Late checkouts are offered subject to availability.
  • ii.The palace is a non-smoking property. Smoking is welcome on the outdoor terraces and within the gardens.
  • iii.We observe quiet hours from 10:00 pm to 7:00 am throughout the grounds.
  • iv.Guests are responsible for any damage to furnishings, artworks, and heritage fabric of the palace.
§ 04

Accessibility

Six of our rooms are adapted for guests with reduced mobility, featuring step-free showers, widened doorways, and accessible furnishings. The main public spaces — reception, ballroom, terrace, and Tilal Bzommar — are all reachable by lift. Please mention any requirements at the time of booking so we can prepare thoughtfully.

§ 05

Sustainability

We are a small property in a fragile landscape. Our kitchen sources 80% of its produce from within 20 kilometers of the palace. We operate on 100% renewable electricity. Greywater irrigates our gardens. Our amenities arrive in refillable ceramic, not single-use plastic. Small measures, practiced consistently.
